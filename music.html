Music Reading

Music

Ambient

Gas - 0095 (1994)

Ambient but house at times. Similar to tracks in other ambient albums where the drums (finally) come in except every track is like that (e.g., 7:39 from 76:14). The towering synths appearing out of nothing in the introduction (second track) is reminiscent of the beginning of Oxygène, but here the composition is much more repetitive. Not to be confused with Wolfgang Voigt’s Gas.

Higher Intelligence Agency and Biosphere - Polar Sequences (1996)

Like going down a rickety mine elevator power. Cold and metallic with rhythms slowly forming.

B12 - Time Tourist (1996)

Features many tracks with driving percussion (e.g., Cymetry) so perhaps veering somewhat away from ambient. But many tracks, such as the opener VOID/COMM are atmospheric. Overall, this sounds like the moment when the beat comes back in an ambient album (e.g., 5 23 from Global Communication) except all the time.

Datacide - Flowerhead (1995)

Slow, low-frequency. Sometimes builds to a muddled dub-techno sound; like Basic Channel with less energy.

Woob - 1194 (1994)

Lots of ups and downs, fading between very repetitive synths and drums (sometimes tribal, sometimes (almost) hip-hop), to field recordings organically blending with melodies and rhythms. Very forward (perhaps non-ambient) at times, but also distant at others. Many “world” instruments/vocals I’d know of if I were smart.

Ben Woods - Moments (2009)

Washing ambience and minimal piano; different from the “stereotypical” ambient, perhaps reminiscent of Tim Hecker.

Volt - Through the Rings (2005)

Melodic; berlin school. The title and album art are, maybe, the best description.

Information - Biomekano (2002)

Loscil - Endless Falls (2010)

The songs slowly build layers of minimally-repetitive sequences. Usually slow and dubby. Percussion consists of clicks washed with reverb. The art matches the mood well.

Oneohtrix Point Never – Returnal (2011)

Moves between noise (e.g., the first track), synthy ambient, and more beatful tracks (e.g., the title track). Has a bit of a synthy 80s vibe going on.

Minimal Electronic

Jan Jelinek – Loop-finding-jazz-records

Pops and clicks and bloops and blips.

DeepChord Presents Echospace - The Coldest Season (2007)

Sprawling dubby collection ranging from minimal techno, to beatless ambient. The album art matches the music.

Blamstrain - Disfold (2006)

Dubby “dark-ambient” techno at times, others a more synthy beatless soundscape. Contains a good dose of field recordings. Some more beat-containing tracks, e.g., the second, are reminiscent to Kenji Yamamoto’s Metroid work. Others, e.g., the third, are similar to Liumin in the combinations of subdued bass drums over washing ambience.

Post Rock

Bedhead - WhatFunLifeWas (1994)

Progresses between somber slowcore-eqsue portion, to a more distorted a grungy sound.

Alternative Rock

Dream Syndicate - Days of Wine and Roses (1982)

Sometimes clear, often noisy guitar, repetitive bass, vocals sounding both melodic and like spoken word.

House of Love - The Creation Recordings (1988–1986)

Big Star - Number 1 Record (1972)

’70s take on ’60s folk-rock.

Shoegaze

Curve - Doppelgänger (1992)

Dense shoegaze guitars, female vocals, and electronic drums.

Medicine – Shot Forth Self Living (1991)

My Bloody Valentine - Isn’t Anything (1988)

Compared to Loveless: the guitars are less dense, the vocals more human; but everything is familiar.

Post Punk

The Sound - Jeopardy (1980)

Forward and repetitive, almost krauty, bass parts; often distorted sometimes jangly guitars. Contains the occasional synth and saxophone.

Josef K - The Only Fun in Town (1981)

Jangly guitars, wobbly-pitched vocals. Muffled punky production, sounding, particularly the vocals, like it’s coming through your neighbors wall. Reminiscent of The Feelies’ Crazy Rhythms without the crystal-clean guitars.

Make Up - Save Yourself (1990)

(Make Me A) Feelin’ Man has a more post-punk sound with upfront distorted guitars. However, the rest have a more soul/funk sound with electric organs, and trumpets. You can compare Ian Svenonius’ singing to soul but really its a sound of its own; perhaps in part due to the warm garage-rock style production.

Magazine - Real Life (1978)

Mix of synths and punky guitars. Vocals reminiscent of Count Chocula.

Slowcore

Mazzy Star - So Tonight That I Might See (1993)

Folksy, bluesy, (sometimes) noisey, reverby female vocals.

Pedro the Lion - It’s Hard to Find a Friend (1998)

Some tracks acoustic, some with a distorted guitar, one percussed by a metronome. Mix of slow-and-sparse and fast-ish-and-full tracks.

Twee

Tiger Trap - Tiger Trap (1995)

Fairly high energy on most tracks. The occasional inflection on vocals adds (e.g., on Words and Smiles) adds to the sentimental lyrics.

Heavenly - Heavenly vs. Satan (1991)

Smooth female vocals, jangly/distorted guitar; saccharine lyrics.

Stoner Rock

Red Giant - Ultra-Magnetic Glowing Sound (1998)

Folk

Syd Barret – The Madcap Laughs (1970)

Psychedelicy folk rock. Quite quirky.

Jazz

West Montgomery - The Incredible Jazz Guitar of West Montgomery (1960)

Very non-abrasive, reminds me of Bossa Nova at times.

Sun Ra and his Arkestra - Jazz in Silhouette (1959)

Complex and “jazzy jazz;” some tracks feature “non-standard” instrumentation and chanting (e.g., Ancient Aiethopia).

Dub Reggae

Dubclash - Dubclash (2002)

Instrumental, minimal, bass heavy.

Bush Chemists - Light Up Your Spliff (1996)

Some vocals, constantly changing effects, and a variety of different sounds over the tracks.

Tommy McCook & The Supersonics – Pleasure Dub (1976)

“Vocals” are often a single reverby section of what sounds like a word.

“Electronic” Dub

Mikkel Metal – Close Selections (2005)

Minimal, almost beatless at times. Uses very atmospheric percussive sounds, like someone playing ping pong in a cavern, similar to Monolake’s Momentum; however, this is much more laid back, and quirky at times.

Hip Hop

Count Bass D - Dwight Spitz (2002)

Choppy samples. Songs themselves are also choppy, many clocking in at just over one minute. Some tracks feature Doom.

Thug Rap

Cormega - The Realness (2001)

Trip Hop

Gotan Project - La Revancha del Tango (2001)

Blends of analog (accordion, guitar, piano) and electronic (mostly drums) instruments in a trip-hop fashion but far away from, say, Portishead.

Nightmares on Wax - In a Space Outta Sound (2006)

Some mostly instrumental tracks, others with vocals. Tempos are low but this never becomes brooding like, e.g., Portishead. Short segments of the tracks are minimal, but things progress often and are layered. This, along with the spattering of vocals, keeps things from sinking into the background.

Massive Attack - Mezzanine (1998)

Dark and almost ambient at times with contributions from reggae artist Horace Andy.

DJ Signify - Of Cities (2009)

Dark, mostly instrumental. The only vocals are Aesop Rock on two tracks. Often drifts leftfield timbre-wise, e.g., on Delight to the Sadist, featuring, at first, almost droning synths and electronic percussion.

Cibo Matto - Viva! La Woman (1996)

Sonically and lyrically quirky. Lyrics are abstract and bordering on nonsensical; some tracks are more emotional (Sugar Water), others more fun (Candy Man).

UNKLE - Psyence Fiction (1998)

DJ Shadow production, somewhat similar to Entroducing. Features a variety of vocalists and rappers.

Downtempo

Ulrich Schnauss – A Strangely Isolated Place (2003)

Shoegaze (think Slowdive/My Bloody Valentine) inspired electronic music. It’s subtle in that the songs are not just electronic covers of shoegaze. The influence can be heard in the timbre of some instruments, e.g., the synth almost sounding like feedback two minutes into track two; or, in the drums (compare those in the intro to A Letter From Home to those in the first few seconds of My Bloody Valentine’s Soon). Overall, much of the wall-of-sound distortion is replaced with softer synths.

Kruder & Dorfmeister - The K&D Sessions (1998)

Dubby and blunted. Often repetitive similar to, say, Endtroducing but in a very different style.

Breakbeat

Dimensional Holofonic Sound - Attention Earth People (2002)

Space themed, technoy at times. Quirky educational spoken-word samples.

Djinji Brown - Sirround Sound (2002)

Latin tinged at times, particularly the first four tracks.

Drum and Bass

Amon Tobin - Permutation (1996)

Still often uses the high-speed drum-and-bass percussion but often with different instruments than in a typical breakbeat; much more “jazz.” Less energy and darker than, say, Hard Normal Daddy.

Tremolite - un-LP (2008)

Sometimes drum-and-bass and sometimes breakbeat, features the ocasional hip-hop spoken-word sample
